Never give up on the disc. A Frisbee disc is catchable until it touches the ground. A disc varies in distance, speed, and height depending on the power, spin, and angle. If a disc seems uncatchable because it went over your head or it is far away from you, there is a good chance that it still is because it may stay afloat for much longer than expected. To us, this is the most important tip to get you started on Ultimate.

CHASE IT

Run to the disc. Sometimes when someone throws the disc to you, you probably will just stand there and wait for the disc to come to you. Then, the moment you catch the disc, a defender will come and block it. This is a common problem for most beginners. Always get close to the thrown disc as much as possible to ensure that the defender can’t block the throw.

PATIENCE IS KEY

Take your time. When you have the disc, just know you have 10 full seconds to make a decision on who to throw it to. Clear your mind and read the field calmly; don’t always rush to throw the disc to someone. Use these 10 seconds wisely.

PROFFESIONAL'S CATCH

Catch the disc with 2 hands as much as possible. Make sure to always use the “pancake” catch if possible because a one-handed catch is always risky. Dropping the disc is a normal occurrence, even in crucial situations, so using the other hand will always bring the success of a catch higher. Only use the one-handed catch when you really need those extra inches to catch the disc.
